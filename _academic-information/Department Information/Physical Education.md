<h4><strong>Programme Design in alignment with MOE Syllabus</strong></h4>
<p>Developing <strong>Committed Participants </strong>who demonstrate a conscious
decision and action to pursue personal excellence and are a role-model
to their family and friends, in leading an active and healthy lifestyle.</p>
<p>In<strong> Truth</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Intellectual dimension</strong>- Pursuing the Truth in deepening
understanding in the discipline of Physical Education and Sports Science.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Moral dimension</strong>- We seek to cultivate relationships through
learning together in the subject, grounded in trust, respect, with a common
purpose to be healthier in the pursuit of excellence.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>In<strong> Love</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Communal</strong> - Exercising love by role-modeling and leading
their peers and family in leading an active and healthy lifestyle.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Individual</strong>- Exercising the choice to be disciplined in
body as an expression of love of oneself.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>By the end of Pre-University 2, students in CJC will have experienced
at least 1 recreational competition organized by the school. In addition,
they will develop and enact a plan for self or others to work towards a
healthier lifestyle through our fitness and gym module.</p>

<h3><strong>MOE Syllabus</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Expectations of MOE Pre-U Syllabus</strong>
</p>
<p>At least 16 hours of curriculum time per module that will develop the
ability of the student to participate independently, safely and with enough
competence to be a participant at a recreational level;</p>
<p>Schools should offer at least three physical activities for every student.</p>
<p>Include learning outcomes which are aligned to the six goals of Physical
Education (through formative assessment);</p>
<p><strong>Goal 1:</strong> Acquire a range of motor skills to participate
in a variety of physical activities.
<br><strong>Goal 2:</strong> Understand and apply movement concepts, principles
and strategies in a range of physical activities.
<br><strong>Goal 3:</strong> Demonstrate safe practices during physical and
daily activities with respect to themselves, others and the environment.
<br><strong>Goal 4:</strong> Display positive personal and social behaviour
across different experiences
<br><strong>Goal 5:</strong> Acquire and maintain health-enhancing fitness
through regular participation in physical activities.
<br><strong>Goal 6:</strong> Enjoy and value the benefits of living a physically
active and healthy life.</p>
